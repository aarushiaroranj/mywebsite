Winston Leonard Spencer-Churchill

Winston Leonard Spencer-Churchill born in Blenheim Palace, United Kingdom on November 30, 1874, was Great Britain’s 6th prime minister. As a child, Churchill was not brought with love that every child should get in their childhood. Churchill was brought up with his nanny and spent most of his time in Boarding Schools. His parents Lord Randolph Henry Spencer-Churchill (Conservative politician) and Jennie Jerome (American-born British socialite) were both a great couple but distant parents and were the reason why Churchill never got the love he deserved being a young and innocent child. Well, Churchill wasn’t that innocent but was a naughty and rebellious child which caused him to switch to multitudinous boarding schools. The strict discipline in these schools didn’t match Churchill’s independent spirit and rebellious nature. Finally, Winston Churchill began growing up and switching schools trying to find out what would suit him. Later after the disastrous school experience, Churchill served in the British Army and worked as a writer. As he served in the military Churchill became known for his military leadership ability. Later May 10, 1940, the prime minister Neville Chamberlain had finally made the public lose confidence in him one incident after another. The House of Commons had impeached him and decided to replace Chamberlain with Winston Leonard Spencer-Churchill. Churchill has been appointed two terms as prime minister! Today he is honored for leading Great Britain through WWII and having a successful outcome of victory. In WWII Winston Churchill specifically was famous for always refusing to give in or surrender. For example, when Germany began bombing Great Britain Winston Churchill refused to surrender to Germany. He made the Great Britain citizens adjusting to houses bombed every day no matter how bad but would never give in to Germany especially still with such little defense. Therefore, Winston Leonard Spencer-Churchill led a good life and died at the age of 90 in Kensington, London, the United Kingdom on January 24, 1965.

Ioseb Besarionis dze Jughashvili in other words known as Joseph Stalin was born in Gori, Georgia on December 18, 1878, serving as the dictator of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics. As a child, Stalin grew up in a poor environment. His dad, Vissarion Dzhugashvili, a poor shoemaker was abusive and often drunk. One day he left the house when Joseph was just five years old. He left his wife, Yekaterina alone to raise three kids. “For the rest of Stalin's childhood, Joseph and Yekaterina lived in the home of a priest”. Stalin got educated partly by random strangers in the priest home. They tried to make Stalin well educated so when he’d grow up he wouldn’t be poor like them. After Yekaterina sent Joseph to a church school in 1888 when he was nine. Six years later he graduated. In school, Joseph had always been one of the few toppers in his class.  After graduating “he applied for and won a small scholarship to the Tiflis Theological Seminary, where he enrolled in September 1894. Yekaterina worked hard to help him afford the tuition, and she nourished a strong hope that her son would become a priest.” Though Stalin never became a priest he rose to power in a very powerful position with his hardwork and dedication. Stalin did this by using a sneaky strategy which was “In the years following the death of Vladimir Lenin in 1924, Stalin rose to become the leader of the Soviet Union. After growing up in Georgia, Stalin conducted discreet activities for the Bolshevik Party for twelve years before the Russian Revolution of 1917”. Joseph Stalin was the dictator of the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics from 1929 to 1953. He had been a dictator, general secretary, and premier of the Soviet Union. “And also, in general, was a politician, activist, and a soldier”. Today he is honored for leading Russia through all the hard times during WWII and having a successful outcome of victory with the allies even though being such a ruthless/cruel dictator (killing all his political enemies). In WWII Joseph Stalin had the role of making a turning point in the war. While the Battle of Stalingrad (Soviet Army fighting German Army) was happening most of the Soviet soldiers had been killed or captured. And without any help of the Allied powers, Joseph Stalin came up with the idea of using the ruined buildings to their advantage. They would use those buildings as forts so they could fire their weapons. That pushed back the Germans and anyways the German soldiers couldn’t take the harsh cold without any supplies so that made Germany lay off Russia. Germany had surrendered to Russia without the help of the Allies. Therefore, Joseph Stalin led a good life and died at the age of 74 in Kuntsevo Dacha on March 5, 1953.

Benito Amilcare Andrea Mussolini born in Predappio, Italy on July 29, 1883, was Italy’s 40th prime minister during WWII. As a child, Mussolini grew up in a pretty inspiring environment to help him towards his destiny of being Italy’s prime minister. His father, Alessandro Mussolini, a dedicated politician and socialist before a blacksmith was Benito’s inspiration. His mother, Rosa Maltoni, was a Catholic school teacher. When Mussolini was young his father taught him all about politics. This gave him a head start towards his career. Because of his dad, Mussolini from the very start became to be known as a smart child but was known for being a very abusive and violent child. “This violent manor could be considered an early development of Mussolini's later-life fascist ideals”. Because of this violent manner of Mussolini faced many problems throughout life especially in schools.“Benito would pinch other children in church, and throw rocks at them outside. In 1894, Benito stabbed a fellow student with a pocket knife, resulting in his immediate expulsion. He had several girlfriends, to whom he treated with utmost disrespect and controlling nature”. As Mussolini grew he switched his schools many times. Later on, Mussolini graduated from college and got the job of a teacher being he didn’t stay in that job for long. Mussolini soon fled to Switzerland and did work involving that country’s Socialist Party. Later in 1909, Mussolini moved to Austria-Hungary to do the job of editing of a socialist newspaper but was sent back to Italy for breaking press laws. In Italy he became the editor for another socialist newspaper and afterward spent 6 months in jail. During that time he wrote an autobiography of his life full of troubles. “Following a stint in the military, Mussolini returned to newspapers and by 1918 called for a dictator to seize control of Italy. After the Treaty of Versailles in 1919 – and his dissatisfaction with it – Mussolini gathered the various fascist groups into a national organization called Fasci Italiani di Combattimento.” Then these groups began to threaten for war and motivated violence against the socialists. At the end of that year, Mussolini decided to run in the election but sadly lost. A couple of days afterward Mussolini got arrested again for overthrowing the government but was let go the next day with no fee. The next election Mussolini, very determined decided to run for it again with a couple of changes. He changed the name of the party to Partito Nazionale Fascista and decided to change the position of what he was running for which was a deputy in Parliament. With Mussolini’s hard work and determinedness, he had won the election! Today he is honored for being a very cruel and ruthless prime minister like Joseph Stalin but worse. He killed/burned all of his enemies and their offices such as the Socialist and Communists offices. People said there was not one single thinking adult with the no fear of being killed by Mussolini. He led WWII in a very harsh way and was one of Germany’s main allies. Mussolini’s role in the war was being Italy’s prime minister which meant taking the right decisions of when to be neutral and when to be allied with the Axis powers. He also helped the enemy which was Germany. Mussolini inspired and gave support to Hitler trying to take over Germany. In my opinion, Mussolini was really a bad guy in the war. “Later Mussolini was overthrown and imprisoned by his former colleagues in the Fascist government”.Therefore, Benito Amilcare Andrea Mussolini led a good life and died at the age of 61 in Giulino, Azzano, Italy on April 28, 1945.

Hideki Tojo

Hideki Tojo born in Kōjimachi, Tokyo, Japan on December 30, 1884, was Japan’s 27th prime minister during WWII. As a child, Tojo was brought up in a very strict/tough environment. His mom, Chitose Tojo, was the daughter of a Buddhist priest and his dad, Hidenori Tojo, a well-respected officer in the Imperial Japanese Army. Following his dad’s footsteps, he was put into a military school named Imperial Japanese Army Academy being taught strict discipline and the world’s harsh reality. Those schools were often very competitive and strict. Many students commit suicide under all that pressure. Tojo might have not been the brightest kid but did have the most amount of common sense so he never even thought about such a horrific idea. As Tojo grew up following his father’s footsteps being the eldest son he attended Army War College. Afterward, Tojo was all grown up and his career for politics had just begun. He started off by joining the military police of Japan’s garrison force in Manchuria. Tojo used this to his advantage, he made his reputation better before he became the Kwantung Army’s chief of staff in 1937. As he rised to power Tojo played a key role in influencing the crowd to be against China. “Seeing the military occupation of Chinese territory as necessary to force the Nationalist Chinese government to collaborate with Japan, he continued to advocate expansion of the conflict in China when he returned to Tokyo in 1938 as army vice minister, rising to army minister in July 1940”.  Since Tojo had such tremendous power/influence he made the decision that would help him be prime minister which was to form an alliance with Germany. He became prime minister in October 1941. Everything was going well at the beginning of the war until it became intense. America threw bombs all over Japan and finally on September 2, 1945, Japan surrendered. The next year Tojo attempted for suicide because instead of getting arrested by the military for the deaths and prisoners of the allies he rather would die. Though Tojo failed and was saved by a doctor he later was arrested and got hanged by the International Military Tribunal.  Today he is honored (not really) for his cruelty and his horrific decision-making skills during WWII. Tojo’s WWII role was to just mess up Japan. As an Army minister, he made Japan join WWII and to aggravate the allies he bombed Pearl Harbor which was a big mistake. In return, they got bombed 10 times worse. All in all, General Tojo made stupid decisions that messed up Japan in WWII. Therefore, Hideki Tojo led a good life and died at the age of 63 in Sugamo Prison, Tokyo, Japan on December 23, 1948.
