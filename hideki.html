Hideki Tojo

Hideki Tojo born in Kōjimachi, Tokyo, Japan on December 30, 1884, was Japan’s 27th prime minister during WWII. As a child, Tojo was brought up in a very strict/tough environment. His mom, Chitose Tojo, was the daughter of a Buddhist priest and his dad, Hidenori Tojo, a well-respected officer in the Imperial Japanese Army. Following his dad’s footsteps, he was put into a military school named Imperial Japanese Army Academy being taught strict discipline and the world’s harsh reality. Those schools were often very competitive and strict. Many students commit suicide under all that pressure. Tojo might have not been the brightest kid but did have the most amount of common sense so he never even thought about such a horrific idea. As Tojo grew up following his father’s footsteps being the eldest son he attended Army War College. Afterward, Tojo was all grown up and his career for politics had just begun. He started off by joining the military police of Japan’s garrison force in Manchuria. Tojo used this to his advantage, he made his reputation better before he became the Kwantung Army’s chief of staff in 1937. As he rised to power Tojo played a key role in influencing the crowd to be against China. “Seeing the military occupation of Chinese territory as necessary to force the Nationalist Chinese government to collaborate with Japan, he continued to advocate expansion of the conflict in China when he returned to Tokyo in 1938 as army vice minister, rising to army minister in July 1940”.  Since Tojo had such tremendous power/influence he made the decision that would help him be prime minister which was to form an alliance with Germany. He became prime minister in October 1941. Everything was going well at the beginning of the war until it became intense. America threw bombs all over Japan and finally on September 2, 1945, Japan surrendered. The next year Tojo attempted for suicide because instead of getting arrested by the military for the deaths and prisoners of the allies he rather would die. Though Tojo failed and was saved by a doctor he later was arrested and got hanged by the International Military Tribunal.  Today he is honored (not really) for his cruelty and his horrific decision-making skills during WWII. Tojo’s WWII role was to just mess up Japan. As an Army minister, he made Japan join WWII and to aggravate the allies he bombed Pearl Harbor which was a big mistake. In return, they got bombed 10 times worse. All in all, General Tojo made stupid decisions that messed up Japan in WWII. Therefore, Hideki Tojo led a good life and died at the age of 63 in Sugamo Prison, Tokyo, Japan on December 23, 1948.
