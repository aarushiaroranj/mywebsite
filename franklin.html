Franklin Delano Roosevelt

Franklin Delano Roosevelt born in Hyde Park, NY on January 30, 1882, was America’s 32nd president. As a child, Roosevelt was born into a wealthy/well-connected family. He grew up in a great environment with everything he needed and all the love he could obtain. Roosevelt was educated by private tutors and elite schools but when Roosevelt went to college things changed. In college, Roosevelt began to admire his cousin Theodore Roosevelt who became president during that time. But when Franklin was young he didn’t even dream about becoming a four-term American president. Then in college, he began to focus on law and politics. After Roosevelt tried many to gain publicity and win presidential elections. And as soon as he knew it with all his hard work he rose to the top. He became a four-term American president! Today he is honored for relieving the depression with his ambitious new deal programs and his enthusiastic spirit. Although Roosevelt is also honored for helping the allies during WWII. He offered supplies and financial support to China and the Soviet Union since Japan was attacking China and Germany was attacking Russia. Roosevelt also made remarkable decisions and strategies such as choosing to join the war, choosing to join the allies. and getting revenge on Japan for the pearl harbor attack. Therefore, Franklin Delano Roosevelt led a good life and died at the age of  63 on April 12, 1945, in Warm Springs, Georgia.

                                Interesting Facts

  1. At the age of 8, Roosevelt developed the passion of collecting Stamps which he did life-long.
  2. Roosevelt had the record of being the longest American president.
  3. Roosevelt was diagnosed with polio at the age of 39 in 1921

                        WWII Quote

One interesting quote I found that Roosevelt said related to WWII was “I have seen war. I have seen war on land and sea. I have seen blood running from the wounded. I have seen men coughing out their gassed lungs. I have seen the dead in the mud. I have seen cities destroyed. I have seen 200 limping, exhausted men come out of line—the survivors of a regiment of 1,000 that went forward 48 hours before. I have seen children starving. I have seen the agony of mothers and wives. I hate war.” In this quote, it conveys to us Roosevelt can understand the pain of the Americans.
