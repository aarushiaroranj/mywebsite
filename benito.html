Andrea Mussolini

Benito Amilcare Andrea Mussolini born in Predappio, Italy on July 29, 1883, was Italy’s 40th prime minister during WWII. As a child, Mussolini grew up in a pretty inspiring environment to help him towards his destiny of being Italy’s prime minister. His father, Alessandro Mussolini, a dedicated politician and socialist before a blacksmith was Benito’s inspiration. His mother, Rosa Maltoni, was a Catholic school teacher. When Mussolini was young his father taught him all about politics. This gave him a head start towards his career. Because of his dad, Mussolini from the very start became to be known as a smart child but was known for being a very abusive and violent child. “This violent manor could be considered an early development of Mussolini's later-life fascist ideals”. Because of this violent manner of Mussolini faced many problems throughout life especially in schools.“Benito would pinch other children in church, and throw rocks at them outside. In 1894, Benito stabbed a fellow student with a pocket knife, resulting in his immediate expulsion. He had several girlfriends, to whom he treated with utmost disrespect and controlling nature”. As Mussolini grew he switched his schools many times. Later on, Mussolini graduated from college and got the job of a teacher being he didn’t stay in that job for long. Mussolini soon fled to Switzerland and did work involving that country’s Socialist Party. Later in 1909, Mussolini moved to Austria-Hungary to do the job of editing of a socialist newspaper but was sent back to Italy for breaking press laws. In Italy he became the editor for another socialist newspaper and afterward spent 6 months in jail. During that time he wrote an autobiography of his life full of troubles. “Following a stint in the military, Mussolini returned to newspapers and by 1918 called for a dictator to seize control of Italy. After the Treaty of Versailles in 1919 – and his dissatisfaction with it – Mussolini gathered the various fascist groups into a national organization called Fasci Italiani di Combattimento.” Then these groups began to threaten for war and motivated violence against the socialists. At the end of that year, Mussolini decided to run in the election but sadly lost. A couple of days afterward Mussolini got arrested again for overthrowing the government but was let go the next day with no fee. The next election Mussolini, very determined decided to run for it again with a couple of changes. He changed the name of the party to Partito Nazionale Fascista and decided to change the position of what he was running for which was a deputy in Parliament. With Mussolini’s hard work and determinedness, he had won the election! Today he is honored for being a very cruel and ruthless prime minister like Joseph Stalin but worse. He killed/burned all of his enemies and their offices such as the Socialist and Communists offices. People said there was not one single thinking adult with the no fear of being killed by Mussolini. He led WWII in a very harsh way and was one of Germany’s main allies. Mussolini’s role in the war was being Italy’s prime minister which meant taking the right decisions of when to be neutral and when to be allied with the Axis powers. He also helped the enemy which was Germany. Mussolini inspired and gave support to Hitler trying to take over Germany. In my opinion, Mussolini was really a bad guy in the war. “Later Mussolini was overthrown and imprisoned by his former colleagues in the Fascist government”.Therefore, Benito Amilcare Andrea Mussolini led a good life and died at the age of 61 in Giulino, Azzano, Italy on April 28, 1945.
