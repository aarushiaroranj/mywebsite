Winston Leonard Spencer-Churchill

Winston Leonard Spencer-Churchill born in Blenheim Palace, United Kingdom on November 30, 1874, was Great Britain’s 6th prime minister. As a child, Churchill was not brought with love that every child should get in their childhood. Churchill was brought up with his nanny and spent most of his time in Boarding Schools. His parents Lord Randolph Henry Spencer-Churchill (Conservative politician) and Jennie Jerome (American-born British socialite) were both a great couple but distant parents and were the reason why Churchill never got the love he deserved being a young and innocent child. Well, Churchill wasn’t that innocent but was a naughty and rebellious child which caused him to switch to multitudinous boarding schools. The strict discipline in these schools didn’t match Churchill’s independent spirit and rebellious nature. Finally, Winston Churchill began growing up and switching schools trying to find out what would suit him. Later after the disastrous school experience, Churchill served in the British Army and worked as a writer. As he served in the military Churchill became known for his military leadership ability. Later May 10, 1940, the prime minister Neville Chamberlain had finally made the public lose confidence in him one incident after another. The House of Commons had impeached him and decided to replace Chamberlain with Winston Leonard Spencer-Churchill. Churchill has been appointed two terms as prime minister! Today he is honored for leading Great Britain through WWII and having a successful outcome of victory. In WWII Winston Churchill specifically was famous for always refusing to give in or surrender. For example, when Germany began bombing Great Britain Winston Churchill refused to surrender to Germany. He made the Great Britain citizens adjusting to houses bombed every day no matter how bad but would never give in to Germany especially still with such little defense. Therefore, Winston Leonard Spencer-Churchill led a good life and died at the age of 90 in Kensington, London, the United Kingdom on January 24, 1965.
